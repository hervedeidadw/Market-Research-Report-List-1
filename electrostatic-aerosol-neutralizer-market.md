<p><p>The electrostatic aerosol neutralizer market comprises two primary types: radioactive and nonradioactive neutralizers. Radioactive neutralizers utilize isotopes to ionize air, effectively neutralizing charged aerosol particles. They are often employed in specialized settings due to regulatory constraints. Nonradioactive neutralizers, on the other hand, use alternative technologies such as electrical discharge or ionizers to achieve aerosol neutralization without radioactive materials. This category is more widely accepted and employed across various industries, including healthcare, clean rooms, and air purification systems.</p></p>
