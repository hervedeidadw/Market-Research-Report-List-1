<p><p>The gene editing tools market includes several advanced technologies: Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFNs), TALENs (Transcription Activator-Like Effector Nucleases), and the CRISPR-Cas system. ZFNs utilize engineered zinc finger proteins to induce double-strand breaks at specific DNA sequences. TALENs, similar to ZFNs, use customizable DNA-binding domains for precise targeting. The CRISPR-Cas system has gained prominence for its simplicity and efficiency, enabling precise genetic modifications. Each tool offers unique advantages, catering to diverse applications in research, agriculture, and therapeutics.</p></p>
