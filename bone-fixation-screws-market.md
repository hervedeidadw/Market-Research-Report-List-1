<p><p>The bone fixation screws market encompasses three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws are widely used for their strength and cost-effectiveness but may pose corrosion issues. Titanium screws are lightweight and biocompatible, making them ideal for sensitive applications, particularly in orthopedic surgery. Bioabsorbable screws are designed to dissolve over time, eliminating the need for removal surgery, making them suitable for pediatric applications and soft tissue repair. Each type caters to specific medical needs and preferences.</p></p>
