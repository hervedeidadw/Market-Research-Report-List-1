<p><p>The Styrene Monomer (SM) market can be categorized into two primary production methods: the Ethylbenzene Dehydrogenation Method and the Ethylbenzene Oxidation Method. The Dehydrogenation method involves converting ethylbenzene into styrene through the removal of hydrogen, making it energy-efficient but requiring high temperatures. Conversely, the Oxidation method utilizes ethylbenzene and air to produce styrene, offering a relatively straightforward process. Both methods have distinct advantages, impacting production costs, efficiency, and overall market dynamics in the styrene industry.</p></p>
